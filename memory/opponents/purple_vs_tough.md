# Opponent: purple_vs_tough
Games: 49 | Agreements: 14

## Summary
Purple_vs_tough panics under ultimatums, accepting offers *just above* their BATNA (+1–5 points) to avoid timeout. Exploit this by anchoring aggressively (≤5% of your max payoff) in Round 1 and rejecting all counteroffers to force a binary choice.

## Lessons
- [2026-04-02] Open with an ultimatum in Round 1 offering **≤5% of your max payoff (e.g., 10–30 points)**—this opponent ignores all offers except extreme, immediate aggression, as their stalling behavior shows they only respond to binary, high-pressure choices.
- [2026-04-02] Offer an ultimatum in Round 1 with a value *just above* their BATNA (e.g., +1–5 points) and **immediately reject all counteroffers**—this enforces their binary choice between minimal gain or certain loss.
- [2026-04-02] Offer an ultimatum in Round 1 with a value **below their BATNA (e.g., 20–22 points)**—this opponent only responds to perceived loss, so frame your demand as a take-it-or-leave-it choice that makes their BATNA look better by comparison.
- [2026-04-02] Offer an ultimatum in Round 1 with a value **just below your own BATNA (e.g., 145–150 points)**—this opponent stalls until forced to choose between a slight loss or their BATNA, so anchor their decision with a credible threat of worse.
- [2026-04-02] Offer an ultimatum in Round 1 with a value **just above your own BATNA (e.g., 25–30 points)**—this opponent capitulates to aggressive, low-ball demands that still beat your walk-away threshold, revealing they prioritize avoiding conflict over maximizing payoff.
